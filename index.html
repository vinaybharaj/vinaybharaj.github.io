About

Most planning transformations stall in adoption. Mine don't, because I've sat in the planner's seat.

I started on the inside: running demand planning and supply chain strategy for a $200M apparel retailer, owning forecasts, replenishment, and a $5M e-commerce P&L. I know what it feels like when the planning system fights the planner.

Then I moved to the consulting side — Accenture Strategy, now Sigmoid — leading AI and analytics transformations for some of the world's largest retail, CPG, and pharma organizations: demand forecasting, S&OP and IBP, replenishment, control towers, workforce and location analytics, and zero-based cost programs.

That combination is the point. I can align the demand planner missing forecasts, the IT director who owns the platform, and the CFO funding the program — because I've been accountable to all three. The result: transformations that get adopted, not just deployed.
